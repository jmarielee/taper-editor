Based on your results, you have a solid foundation. Finishing in 1:51:16 means you aren't starting from scratch—you're already a triathlete. Looking at your splits, here are the biggest opportunities:

Discipline	Time	Place	Assessment
Swim (0.5 mi)	19:38	65th	Good. Maintain and improve efficiency.
T1	3:08	93rd	Easy place to gain 30–60 seconds.
Bike (11 mi)	47:08	117th	Biggest opportunity. Improve endurance and power.
T2	1:54	102nd	Can improve with practice.
Run (5K)	39:28	132nd	Largest opportunity. Improving run fitness will make the biggest difference.
Your goal should be to finish around 1:38–1:42, which is very realistic with consistent training over the next year.

Training Philosophy

5 training days/week

2 recovery days/week (Monday & Friday)

3 workouts include more than one discipline ("brick" workouts)

One longer endurance session every weekend

Build for three weeks, recover one week

Effort zones:

Easy: conversational pace

Steady: comfortably hard

Race Pace: the effort you want to hold on race day

Intervals: difficult but controlled

Open Water Practice

Beginning about 6 weeks before the race, aim to swim in open water every other week if conditions are safe. Practice:

Sighting every 6–8 strokes

Swimming in a straight line

Deep-water starts

Swimming while slightly winded

Staying relaxed if bumped by other swimmers
